About Me

Quality Assurance

Graduating with a Bachelors in Computer Science at Long Beach State University, I originally planned on becoming a software developer. After college, I went into Quality Assurance as a stepping stone and realized I liked it more than development. My strong analytical skills and high attention to detail were better suited for QA as well. More recently, I've been incredibly interested with automation testing and becoming an SDET, finding that it's the perfect combination of QA and scripting.

Web Development

Following the lay off of my entire previous company, and a couple months of unsuccessful job searching, I wanted to start a big project that I had visualized a couple years ago but could never find the time to do. I wanted to create a website for myself. I felt this was a great way to combine both the creative and technical side of me, while also being a solid example of my computer science skills for my job search.

My webiste/portfolio

Started: ~June 22, 2023.

Rather than create it through a web builder like Wix, I decided I wanted to build this website from scratch with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I watched multiple tutorials on YouTube and used a bootstrap to help me hit the ground running with a designed and functional outline. I also used Google's "Bard" (their version of ChatGPT) to assist with any quick looking up of references or syntax. I backed up my work with a Github repository, and used that for version control. I used a combination of paint, an online icon maker, google images, and Lunapic.com to help create most of the graphics on my website. Once I was happy with the MVP (Minimum Viable Product), I invested into Hostinger to be able to host my website on a domain along with necessary features. Currently, I am still customizing it to include more of what I'd like to present, and will add more features to it later.
