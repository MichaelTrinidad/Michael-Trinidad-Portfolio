About Me

Quality Assurance

Graduating with a Bachelors in Computer Science at Long Beach State University, I originally planned on becoming a software developer. After college, I went into Quality Assurance as a stepping stone into a development career. As I worked as a tester, I started to realize I naturally have strong analytical skills and a high attention to detail, which pointed to the fact that QA was more my thing than software development. More recently, I've been incredibly interested with automation testing, as that seems to be the best of both worlds: QA and coding.

Web Development

Following the lay off of my entire previous company, and a couple months of unsuccessful job searching, I wanted to start a big project that I had visualized a couple years ago but could never find the time to do. I wanted to create a website for myself. I felt this was a great way to combine both the creative and technical side of me, while also being a solid example of my computer science skills for my job search. Although I have come a long way with the website already, it is not nearly close to the vision I had, and will continuously be a work in progress.

Shuffling

In 2008/2009, a friend told me about a new dance that he was getting into, called Shuffling. Wanting to be part of the adventure, I looked into Shuffling myself, saw videos of "Moonboy" and "Francis", and immediately got hooked into the dance. During this time, I was also overweight, so stomping my feet to 150 bpm songs was a great workout, and was actually how I lost my first 15 lbs. Since then, I've gone to raves, shows, events, and even other states to shuffle and perform. I've participated in multiple tournaments, both as a battler and a judge, and have taught many classes and private lessons. I've also hosted sessions in different areas around LA so shufflers would have a safe spot to dance, and to also connect and network with each other.

My webiste/portfolio

Started: ~June 22, 2023.

Rather than create it through a web builder like Wix, I decided I want to build it from scratch with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I watched multiple tutorials on YoutTube and used a bootstrap to help me hit the ground running with a designed and functional outline. I also used Google's "Bard" (their version of ChatGPT) to assist with any quick looking up of references or syntax. I backed up my work with a Github repository, and used that for version control. I used a combination of paint, an online icon maker, google images, and Lunapic.com to help create most of the graphics on my website. Once I was happy with the MVP (Minimum Viable Product), I invested into Hostinger to be able to host my website on a domain along with necessary features. Currently, I am still customizing it to include more of what I'd like to present, and will add more features to it later.
